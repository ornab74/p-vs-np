Statement:
On focused instances H<C*> with a unique witness clique C*, any circuit that
computes L_mix^hash correctly must be invariant under resampling any block phi_i
for i not in C*, except with negligible probability.

Semantic Fact:
On H<C*>,
  L(H<C*>, Phi, S) = AND over j in C* of SAT(phi_j),
which is completely independent of phi_i for i not in C*.

Proof Outline:
1) Condition on UNIQ and on acceptance with verified witness C*.
2) Fix any block i not in C*.
3) Consider two instances differing only in phi_i:
      I  = (H<C*>, Phi, S)
      I' = (H<C*>, Phi^(i <- i'), S)
   where phi_i' is an independent resample.
4) By semantics, L(I) = L(I').
5) If the circuit flips output between I and I' with probability >= epsilon,
   then we can sample such pairs in polynomial time and obtain explicit
   disagreements with the language on Omega(epsilon) measure.
6) Therefore correctness implies:
      Pr[f(I) != f(I')] <= negligible.

Amplification:
Repeating the resampling test r = Theta(log(1/delta)) times ensures uniform stability:
any block with non-negligible influence is detected with high probability.

Conclusion:
Correctness implies block resampling invariance on all off-clique blocks.
